Philosophy

“HR should function more like an engineering discipline than an administrative one.”

Individuality Openness Efficacy Winning Humility + sense of humor

I came into HR through behavioral science, and that lens has never left. People are the most complex system any organization operates — and most HR functions treat them like a cost center to be managed, not a capability to be designed.

My operating premise is simple: the organizations that win are the ones that build people systems intentionally. That means clear governance. Reliable data. Analytics that drive decisions, not just decorate slide decks. And leadership models where the function doesn’t rise and fall with whoever’s in the seat.

I’ve spent my career being somewhat of a renegade about this — pushing back on HR as reactive service delivery, advocating for People functions that hold themselves to the same rigor as Finance or Engineering. Not because it’s trendy, but because it works.

The emergence of AI in workforce management doesn’t change that premise — it accelerates it. The organizations that have their data, roles, and systems right will absorb AI with genuine leverage. The ones that don’t will absorb it as another layer of complexity on top of a broken foundation.
